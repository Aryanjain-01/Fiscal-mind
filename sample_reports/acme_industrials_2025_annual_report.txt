ACME Industrials Annual Report 2025

Business overview
ACME Industrials manufactures automation equipment for factories, warehouses, and energy infrastructure customers. Management reports that demand improved across North America and India during fiscal 2025, while European orders remained uneven because customers delayed large capital projects.

Revenue and margin
Revenue increased to $4.82 billion in fiscal 2025 from $4.31 billion in fiscal 2024, driven by higher shipment volumes in robotics, price increases in aftermarket parts, and stronger service contract renewals. Gross margin improved to 38.4% from 36.9% as the company benefited from lower freight costs and better factory utilization. Operating margin expanded to 17.2% compared with 15.8% in the prior year.

Profitability
Net income was $612 million, compared with $521 million in fiscal 2024. Diluted earnings per share were $4.18, up from $3.51. EBITDA was $914 million, reflecting revenue growth and disciplined spending in selling, general, and administrative expenses.

Cash flow and balance sheet
Operating cash flow was $742 million and free cash flow was $586 million. Capital expenditures were $156 million, mainly for plant automation and software systems. Cash and equivalents were $438 million at year end. Total debt was $1.24 billion, and management stated that leverage remains within the target range.

Segment performance
Robotics revenue grew 18% to $1.96 billion because warehouse automation customers accelerated deployments. Industrial Controls revenue grew 7% to $1.71 billion, supported by pricing and replacement demand. Energy Systems revenue declined 3% to $1.15 billion because utility customers pushed several projects into fiscal 2026.

Risks
Management identified supply chain disruption, customer concentration in large automation programs, foreign exchange volatility, and delayed industrial capital spending as key risks. The company also noted that AI-enabled robotics products require sustained research and development investment, which could pressure margins if adoption is slower than expected.

Outlook
For fiscal 2026, ACME expects revenue growth of 6% to 8% and operating margin between 17.0% and 18.0%. Management expects free cash flow conversion above 90% of net income if component availability remains stable.
