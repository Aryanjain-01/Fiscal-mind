Nova Retail Holdings Annual Report 2025

Business overview
Nova Retail Holdings operates discount stores and an e-commerce marketplace for household goods, apparel, and packaged food. The company served 42 million active loyalty members at the end of fiscal 2025.

Revenue and comparable sales
Net sales were $12.6 billion in fiscal 2025, an increase of 4.2% from $12.1 billion in fiscal 2024. Comparable store sales increased 2.8%, supported by grocery traffic and private-label products. E-commerce revenue increased 15% to $1.84 billion, but marketplace advertising growth slowed in the fourth quarter.

Profitability
Gross margin was 29.6%, down from 30.2%, because of higher shrink, promotional activity, and a larger mix of grocery sales. Operating income declined to $815 million from $862 million. Net income was $504 million, and diluted earnings per share were $2.76.

Cash flow and capital allocation
Operating cash flow was $921 million. Free cash flow was $417 million after $504 million of capital expenditures for store remodels, logistics automation, and technology upgrades. The company repurchased $250 million of common stock and paid $146 million in dividends.

Balance sheet
Inventory was $2.18 billion at year end, up 6% from the prior year. Total debt was $3.4 billion, including lease liabilities. Cash and equivalents were $312 million. Management plans to reduce inventory growth in fiscal 2026 by tightening seasonal purchasing.

Risks
Nova faces margin pressure from wage inflation, freight costs, inventory shrink, and promotional competition. Consumer demand may weaken if inflation continues to reduce discretionary spending. Cybersecurity incidents could disrupt the e-commerce marketplace or expose customer data.

Outlook
Management expects fiscal 2026 net sales growth of 3% to 5%, comparable store sales growth of 1% to 3%, and operating income growth in the low single digits. Capital expenditures are expected to be approximately $475 million.
